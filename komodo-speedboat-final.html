Questions, answered

The things every traveler asks us.

What's the best month to go?
April through early November is the dry season — calm seas, peak visibility, highest manta density. July–August is peak demand, so book 4–6 weeks ahead. December–March is monsoon; we still run, but route dynamically based on conditions.
Will I actually see a Komodo dragon?
Yes — sightings are reliable year-round on both Komodo and Rinca Islands. Rangers know the active zones. We've never had a group return without multiple close encounters (at a safe distance).
Is snorkel gear included? What about a drone?
Snorkel mask, fins, and life vest are included and sanitized daily. Drone operation requires a permit in Komodo National Park — we can arrange a licensed operator for +IDR 750,000. GoPros are welcome.
What if the weather is bad?
We make the call the evening before based on BMKG (Indonesian Met) data. If we cancel, you receive a full refund or free reschedule. If we re-route mid-day, we optimize for the best remaining stops — zero extra cost.
Can kids come? Non-swimmers?
Yes. We've hosted guests from age 5 to 78. Pink Beach and Taka Makassar have calm shallows; life vests are available for all. Let us know ages at booking so we can prep appropriately.
How do I pay, and how do I reach you the day of?
50% deposit via bank transfer or card to confirm; balance on arrival or online. You get the skipper's direct WhatsApp 48 hours before departure — no call centers, no forms.
Why is it cheaper than GetYourGuide?
Because you're booking the operator directly. OTAs add a 20–30% platform margin on top of our rate. Same boat, same skipper, same crew — just without the middleman.
