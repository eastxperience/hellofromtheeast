Why this experience matters

A different kind of travel day.

Not a volcano-bucket-list rush. A curated act of restoration set against one of Java's most dramatic landscapes.

Drama

A crater lake the color of moon.

Kawah Putih means "white crater." At sunrise, with no one there, the turquoise-white water steams under a skin of mist. It feels less like Earth and more like a scene you'd film.

Healing

Private onsen at 42°C.

Booked for you alone. Walled in volcanic stone, open to the mist and tea slopes. No schedules, no other guests — one full hour that changes what "rest" means.

Terroir

Century-old tea. Tea-smoked trout.

Walk through the Malabar plantation planted in 1896. Lunch at a tea-maker's family home — smoked highland trout, sambal, freshly picked green leaves.

Questions, answered

The things every traveler asks.

Is the crater safe? Smell of sulfur?
Yes — Kawah Putih is a stable tourist-accessible crater. Light sulfur smell at the rim; many guests love it. Masks provided for sensitivity.
How cold is it up there?
6 AM crater: 12–16°C. Bring a light jacket (we also carry spare fleeces). Onsen is 42°C — perfectly calibrated.
Can older travelers manage the walks?
Absolutely. Tea walk is flat, 60 min gentle. Crater has an optional short climb — easy opt-out. We've hosted guests up to age 82.
Any medical contraindications for the onsen?
42°C volcanic pool — not recommended for first-trimester pregnancy, uncontrolled hypertension, or severe cardiac conditions. Tell us beforehand and we'll offer a cooler tea-spring alternative.
Is the lunch negotiable for dietary needs?
Yes — full vegetarian, halal, gluten-free, and kids' menus available. Tell us at booking.
Can this combine with rafting the next day?
Yes. We can package both Bandung experiences across 2 days with one pickup and a central hotel recommendation — ask the concierge.
