Why this experience matters

Everyone goes to Waerebo. Almost nobody goes to Todo.

And Todo is where the Manggarai story actually begins.

Origin

The mother village.

Todo is the ancestral seat of the Manggarai royal lineage. The sacred drums — Tambur Todo — are kept here, woven from human hair and buffalo skin, over 400 years old.

Craft

Sacred songke weavers.

Meet the women who weave songke — the black and gold ceremonial textile. A single cloth takes 6 months. You'll see the loom, the thread, and the symbols only a few on earth still know.

Rare

Cunca Plias waterfall.

A 60-meter curtain in the forest. No Instagram. No crowd. You'll likely be the only outsiders there all day — and the village guide knows the quiet pool beneath it.

What makes it different

Respect-led. Not a staged tour.

We work directly with the Todo council. Pricing, rules, visitor caps — all set by the village elders. 70% of what you pay stays in their economy.

Questions, answered

Respectful travel questions.

Is this a staged cultural show?
No. Todo is a living village. What you see — the weaving, the drums, the cooking — is their everyday life. You are a welcomed guest, not an audience.
What should I wear?
Anything modest — covered shoulders and knees for the sacred compound. We provide a sarong if needed. Comfortable trail shoes for the waterfall walk.
Can I photograph freely?
Outside the sacred compound, yes. Inside, your guide will tell you when to put the camera away. Always ask before photographing a person — most will say yes.
Is it suitable for kids / older travelers?
Yes from age 8+. Waterfall trail is 30 min with gentle descent. Guests up to 75 regularly join. Tell us about anyone with mobility concerns — we adapt.
What's the drive time?
2.5 hours each way on a winding mountain road. We use 4WD with captain seats. Motion-sensitive guests should take medication before.
Can I buy the weaving direct?
Yes — we actively encourage it. Songke pieces range from IDR 800K (small) to IDR 8M (ceremonial). Money goes directly to the weaver, cash or transfer.
What if the village is closed for a ritual?
We reschedule or refund in full. Their calendar always takes precedence — this is what respect-led tourism actually looks like.
